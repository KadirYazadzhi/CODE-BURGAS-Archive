# Merchant

## Description
Once upon a time, there was a young merchant named Georgi who had a small trading business. One day, he decided to take a trip to a distant land to buy some rare goods that he could sell at a high price in his hometown. Georgi had limited funds and couldn't afford to buy all the goods he wanted, but he still wanted to maximize his profit.

As he traveled through the markets of the distant land, he found many different types of goods, each with its own weight and value. Some goods were heavy and of high value, while others were light but of low value. Georgi had limited space in his backpack, so he couldn't buy all the goods he wanted.

Help the merchant by writing a program that selects the goods with which Georgi should fill his backpack so that his profit is maximized.

In gratitude, Georgi will make you a legend in the trading community, inspiring many other young merchants to use the same algorithm to make smart and profitable business decisions.

## Input
From the first line of the standard input, two integers are entered: $n$ and $c$, separated by a space, which correspond to the number of items and the capacity of the merchant's backpack.
On the next $n$ lines from the standard input, two integers are entered: $v$ and $w$, separated by a space, which correspond to the weight and value of each item.

## Output
On a single line of the standard output, the program should output the maximum profit the merchant can receive for the goods in his backpack. The answer is output up to the third decimal place.

## Example

### Input
```
5 15
3 50
5 70
2 30
1 10
4 40
```

### Output
```
200.000
```
